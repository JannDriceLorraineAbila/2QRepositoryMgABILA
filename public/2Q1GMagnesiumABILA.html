1. Basic Objective

The goal is to hit the shuttlecock over the net and land it inside the opponent‚Äôs court while preventing them from returning it.

2. Court and Equipment

  • Court length: 13.4 m
  • Width: Singles ‚Äì 5.18 m; Doubles ‚Äì 6.1 m
  • Net height: 1.55 m at posts, 1.524 m at center
  • Equipment: racket and shuttlecock

3. Types of Play

  • Singles ‚Äì 1 vs 1
  • Doubles ‚Äì 2 vs 2
  • Mixed Doubles ‚Äì 1 male + 1 female per team

4. Scoring System

  • Best of 3 games, each to 21 points
  • Every rally gives 1 point
  • Win by 2 points at 20‚Äì20
  • At 29‚Äì29, next point wins

5. Serving Rules

  • Server and receiver stand diagonally opposite in service courts.
  • Both must keep part of both feet on the ground.
  • Serve must be below the waist, and racket head below hand.
  • Serve must be hit in one continuous motion.
  • Singles uses narrow and long boundaries.
  • Doubles uses wide and short boundaries for serve, wide and long for rally.
  • Serving side alternates courts (right/left) after scoring a point.
  • Receiving side does not change courts unless they win and serve next.

6. Order of Play & Positions

  • Singles: serve from right service court when score is even; left when odd.
  • Doubles: same rule applies.
  • Only serving side changes sides after scoring.

7. Faults

  • Shuttle lands outside court boundaries.
  • Shuttle fails to cross the net.
  • Shuttle touches the ceiling or walls.
  • Player touches the net or invades opponent‚Äôs side.
  • Shuttle is caught or held on racket (no slinging).
  • Double hit: shuttle hit twice by same player before crossing net.
  • Serve above waist height.
  • Racket head above hand during serve.
  • Shuttle lands outside service boundaries during serve.
  • Player deliberately distracts or delays the game.

8. Lets

  • Server serves before receiver is ready.
  • Both server and receiver commit faults at the same time.
  • Shuttle is caught on top of the net or remains suspended after crossing it.
  • Shuttle, after passing over the net, is caught in or on the net.
  • Receiver and server are unsure if the serve was legal.
  • Line judge, umpire, or service judge unable to make a decision.
  • Any unforeseen or accidental disturbance occurs.
  • Shuttle breaks or becomes damaged during play.
  • Play is stopped by the umpire for a valid reason.

9. Change of Ends

  • Players switch sides after the first game.
  • Before the third game (if there is one).
  • In the third game, when one side reaches 11 points.

10. Intervals and Breaks

  • 60-second break at 11 points in each game.
  • 2-minute interval between games.
  • Only coach may advise players during breaks, not during rallies.

11. Conduct and Fair Play

  • Respect opponent and umpire at all times.
  • No shouting or distractions.
  • No damaging the court, shuttle, or racket intentionally.

12. Winning the Match

  • The first player or team to win 2 out of 3 games wins the match.
